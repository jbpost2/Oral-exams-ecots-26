# File Information

These oral exams were given in an undergraduate statistical theory class, generally one of the last courses a statistics major would take.

The first two oral exams (Spring 2026 Exam 1 Oral Component; Spring 2026 Exam 2 Oral Component) were given in a take-home format where students were encouraged to work together on the problems. During the oral exam itself, students were not asked to just “do” the problems for me; instead, they were asked more intentional questions to assess their understanding, as well as some deeper questions to assess their ability to connect ideas together and extend beyond what was exactly taught. 

The last oral exam (Example Oral Final) was given to the students when they showed up for their appointment with me. They were given 45 minutes to prepare outside of my office, and then we had a 30-minute oral exam appointment immediately after. Each exam was designed for the individual student, aiming to point out some areas that they had struggled with during the course, to see if their understanding had improved.
