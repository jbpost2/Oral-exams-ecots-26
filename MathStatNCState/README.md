# ST 502 - Fundamentals of Statistical Inference II

## Oral Discussion #0

This course is a follow-on course from ST-501 (Fundamentals of Statistical Inference I). The schedule of topics for the course is available in the file `ST502-Fall25Schedule.pdf`. 

At the beginning of the course, I wanted to make sure everyone was clear on the background material they needed to know, used similar language in discussion the material, and was prepared for later oral discussions. The initial homework assignment was a final exam from the first course I had given previously (see homework1.pdf and homework1key.pdf). The oral discussion #0 was worth only 3% of the final grade.

- `Discussion_0_Questions.qmd` and `Discussion_0_Questions.pdf` give the list of questions. Two randomly selected 'quick questions' were chosen and both 'more involved questions' were asked.

## Rubric

The grading for the 'quick questions' were done on a four point scale and a six point scale was used for the longer questions.

| Scale | 0 Points | 1 Point | 2 Points | 3 Points | 4 Points | 5 Points | 6 Points |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Four Point Scale** | **No credit**<br>Inability to answer the question correctly. | In between Fair and No credit | **Fair**<br>Answer is nearly correct or is not quite optimal. Obtaining the 'best' answer requires further prompting. | In between Excellent and Fair | **Excellent**<br>Answer is correct and optimal in light of what we learned and defined. No follow up is required to obtain clarity. | — | — |
| **Six Point Scale** | **No credit**<br>Inability to present any understanding of the concepts covered in the question. | In between Fair and No credit | **Fair**<br>Limited ability to articulate the concepts covered in the question, explanations contain inconsistencies and demonstrate limited understanding of the content. | In between Good and Fair | **Good**<br>Ability to articulate the central concepts covered in the question, but errors may occur during the assessment which may cloud understanding articulated during the answer. | In between Excellent and Good | **Excellent**<br>Outstanding ability to articulate the concepts covered in the question. Few if any errors occur. Any errors that do occur are minor and do not cloud the understanding articulated during the answer. |
